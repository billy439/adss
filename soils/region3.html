Farming Region Three - Transition Zone

Soil characteristics

Region Three lies between the high rainfall zones of Regions 1 and 2 and the drier Regions 4 and 5. It covers parts of the Midlands, Masvingo, and south-eastern Zimbabwe. This region experiences moderate rainfall and is suitable for both crop and livestock farming.

Here's a bit more detail about these soil types in Region Three:

Clay loams and sandy clay loams :

These soils are moderately fertile and retain moisture better than sandy soils, supporting both crop production and pastures.

Vertisols and Luvisols:

These are typical in this region. Vertisols are dark, clay-rich soils that expand and contract with moisture, while Luvisols are moderately leached, fertile soils suited to semi-intensive farming.
